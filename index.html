You are not alone. And what you feel matters.

Solicitude is a youth led, non profit mental health initiative based in India. We work to spread awareness, encourage honest emotional expression, and break the stigma around mental well being through education, stories, and community driven efforts.

We do not offer therapy or quick solutions. What we offer is understanding, awareness, and a safe starting point for people who feel unheard, confused, or simply curious about mental health.

Follow our work on Instagram

Why Solicitude Exists

Because silence should not be the default.

What we see around us

Across India, many people struggle quietly with emotions they do not fully understand or feel safe talking about. Mental health is often brushed aside, joked about, or discussed only when things become unbearable.
Young people face constant pressure from academics, family expectations, social comparison, and uncertainty about the future. Yet there are very few spaces where they can speak honestly without fear of being judged or misunderstood.

Why this gap still exists

Most mental health conversations are either too clinical to relate to or too shallow to actually help. When people cannot see themselves in the conversation, they stop engaging altogether.

Solicitude exists to make mental health conversations feel human, relatable, and accessible before silence turns into suffering.

What We Do

Awareness and education rooted in real experiences.

Awareness campaigns

We run focused mental health awareness campaigns through social media and collaborative initiatives. These campaigns are designed to encourage reflection, conversation, and empathy in a way that feels safe and respectful.

Research and surveys

We conduct surveys and contribute to research based discussions to better understand mental health experiences, especially among young people. This helps ensure our work is informed and responsible.

Blogs and educational content

We write blogs and resources that explain psychological ideas and emotional experiences in simple language. The goal is clarity, not complexity.

Interviews and documentaries

We share real stories from real people. Through interviews and documentary style content, we highlight lived experiences so others feel less alone.

Community endeavours and collaborations

We work with individuals and organisations who share our goal of building a more mentally aware and compassionate society.

Who This Is For

If this feels familiar, you are in the right place.

  • People who are struggling mentally and want to feel seen or understood.

  • Students and young adults interested in psychology and mental health.

  • Individuals looking for a safe and non judgemental space to express emotions.

  • People and organisations who want to collaborate on meaningful mental health initiatives.

  • You do not need to have all the answers to be here. Wanting to understand is enough.

How We Approach Mental Health

Care, responsibility, and honesty.

  • We speak about mental health with sensitivity and respect.

  • We share stories only with consent and care.

  • We avoid exaggeration, trends, and misinformation.

  • We are clear about our limits and honest about what we can offer.

  • Trust matters deeply in mental health work, and we treat it seriously.

Why Be Part of Solicitude

Because understanding can change everything.

By being part of Solicitude, you gain access to thoughtful mental health content that is easy to understand. You get to hear real experiences that help reduce shame and isolation. You can participate in awareness campaigns and initiatives if and when you feel ready. You become part of a community that values empathy, learning, and emotional honesty. Sometimes the first step is not healing. It is understanding. We focus on that first.

How To Get Involved

What Happens Next

Nothing overwhelming. Just connection.

Once you follow us, you will start seeing our content regularly.

You can engage quietly, learn at your own pace, or take part when you feel comfortable.

Solicitude grows through people, not numbers. And every person counts.
